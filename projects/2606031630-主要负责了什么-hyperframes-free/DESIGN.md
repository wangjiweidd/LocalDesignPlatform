# Visual Identity: Interview Signal Studio

## Style Prompt

A premium dark editorial video interface for design interview coaching. The video feels like a cinematic strategy room: floating glass documents, focused spotlights, precise answer-logic paths, and controlled camera movement. The voiceover explains the structure while the screen reveals only the currently relevant answer fragment. It should feel fluid, selective, and intentional, not like a filled static page.

## Colors

- Background ink: `#0e1117`
- Panel glass: `rgba(246, 242, 232, 0.92)`
- Text ivory: `#f6f2e8`
- Body ink: `#171a20`
- Signal gold: `#f0c35a`
- Focus cyan: `#7dd3fc`
- Critique red: `#ff5d57`
- Muted graphite: `#8b93a4`

## Typography

- Use one Chinese sans-serif stack throughout: `"PingFang SC", "Heiti SC", "Noto Sans SC", "Microsoft YaHei", sans-serif`.
- Titles are heavy, compressed by wording rather than by mixing fonts.
- Code-like labels may use letter spacing, but still use the same font family.

## Motion Rules

- Long template content appears through a viewport: reveal, pan, and zoom to the relevant fragment.
- Avoid showing the full template at full density in every frame.
- Use focused highlights, cursor lines, and camera shifts to follow the voiceover.
- No looping decorative animation.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use Odin pale paper, hand-drawn stamps, or green teaching-board rails.
- Do not use large static cards filled with all text at once.
- Do not mix serif, sans, and monospace families.
- Do not add random background particles or generic SaaS dashboard cards.
